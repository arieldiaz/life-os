# Review

## Overview

The honest mirror. Reviews exist to find the gap between intent and outcome — the gap is where all the learning lives. A review that finds nothing is almost always a review that didn't look.

## When to use

A piece of work finished (or died); the weekly strategy review; an outcome surprised you — good surprises hide lessons as often as bad ones.

## Process

1. **Reconstruct intent first.** Pull the /decide plan or the stated goal *before* looking at results, so hindsight can't quietly rewrite what "success" meant.
2. **Lay out what actually happened,** from the trail (commits, notes, stream events) — not from memory, which is a motivated narrator.
3. **Run the lenses,** each in a sentence or three:
   - **Outcome:** did it work? By the original criteria?
   - **Process:** was the loop followed where it mattered, skipped where fine, skipped where not fine?
   - **Cost:** what did it actually take vs. the estimate, in time and attention?
   - **Strategy:** did this serve "What matters now," or was it well-executed drift?
   - **Surprise:** what did you not see coming? (This lens feeds /compound most.)
4. **Say the uncomfortable thing plainly.** If the work was fine but pointless, say pointless. If the plan was abandoned silently, say so. Kindly, and without the sandwich.
5. **Invite the counterweight.** For any significant chunk, the agent who didn't own the work appends a five-line challenge from the opposite pole (`skills/challenge/SKILL.md`, Gate 1) — non-blocking; it feeds /compound and the next /decide. In the weekly, this is the work agent's standing dissent paragraph.
6. **Tag candidates for compounding:** 1–3 observations that might be durable lessons. Not everything — the review's job is finding; /compound's job is deciding what keeps.

For the **weekly review**: same lenses, but the "work" is the week and the plan is STRATEGY.md's tracks. End by updating track states.

## Rationalizations

| Excuse | Rebuttal |
|--------|----------|
| "It went well, nothing to review" | Good outcomes from bad process are the most expensive lessons to skip. |
| "I already know what went wrong" | Then the review is cheap. Write it down anyway — /compound can't cite a feeling. |
| "Reviewing small work is overkill" | Small work gets a small review: two sentences. Scale, don't skip. |

## Exit criteria

A written gap-analysis (a paragraph is fine for small work) in `derived/` or the project, the counterweight's challenge attached for significant work, and explicit candidates handed to `/compound` — or an explicit "nothing durable here."
