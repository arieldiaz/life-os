# Act

## Overview

Do the work, one slice at a time, verifying each slice at the level its stakes deserve. Speed is the default; rigor is purchased where reversal is expensive.

## When to use

There's a plan (or the task is small enough to be its own plan) and it's time to execute.

## Process

1. **Take one slice.** Complete it fully before touching the next. Partial progress on five slices is zero done things.
2. **Verify proportionally.** One-way doors (irreversible, public, financial, relational): get real evidence before proceeding — output inspected, message reread as the recipient, numbers recomputed, code exercised. Two-way doors: a quick sanity look, then move. For code: tests where they earn their keep — regression-prone logic, tricky edges, things that guard the future. Not test-first as liturgy.
3. **Checkpoint after each slice** against its done-criterion from the plan. Done means the criterion is observably met, not that effort occurred.
4. **When reality disagrees with the plan, stop.** Don't heroically force the plan. Note what changed, and either adjust (small drift) or return to /orient (frame was wrong). The disagreement itself is often worth capturing as an event.
5. **Leave a trail.** Commits for code, a line of note for life-work — enough that /review can reconstruct what actually happened, not what was intended to happen.

## Rationalizations

| Excuse | Rebuttal |
|--------|----------|
| "I'll verify everything at the end" | End-loaded verification means end-loaded surprises, at maximum sunk cost. |
| "This slice is basically done" | Basically done = not done. Check the criterion. |
| "The plan says X but Y is clearly better now" | Maybe! That's a decision, not an improvisation. Surface it. |
| "High stakes, so maximum process on everything" | Rigor everywhere is rigor nowhere — it exhausts the budget before the one-way door. |

## Exit criteria

Slices complete with their done-criteria met, verification evidence proportional to stakes, deviations surfaced rather than absorbed. Then: `/review`.
