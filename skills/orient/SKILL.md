# Orient

## Overview

Orientation is where judgment happens — connecting the thing in front of you to strategy, memory, and history before any commitment. Most bad decisions are good decisions about the wrong frame. This step buys the right frame.

## When to use

Starting any non-trivial piece of work; something in the stream pulls at you; a situation feels confusing or higher-stakes than it looks; the human says "help me think about X."

## Process

1. **Load context:** read `STRATEGY.md` and `memory/index.md`. Follow index lines into specific lessons if relevant. Check `derived/` and `stream/` for related history.
2. **State the situation in one paragraph,** plainly. If you can't, that's the finding — orient on why.
3. **Interrogate gently.** Ask the human what's actually going on — inline, conversational, ONE question at a time, and stop when marginal questions stop changing the picture (usually 2–4). Never a form, never a popup.
4. **Surface tensions:** where does this conflict with "What matters now" or "Explicitly not doing" in STRATEGY.md? Where has memory seen this shape before?
5. **Name the frames.** Offer 2–3 distinct ways to see the situation, with what each implies. Recommend one and say why.
6. **Write it down** if it's substantial: a short orientation note in `derived/` (provenance header pointing at the relevant stream events).

## Rationalizations

| Excuse | Rebuttal |
|--------|----------|
| "The situation is obvious, skip to planning" | Obvious situations don't get /orient invoked on them. Something prompted this. |
| "Reading memory/strategy is overhead" | It's two short files. Skipping them is how the system fails to compound. |
| "More questions = more thorough" | Past the point of a stable picture, questions are procrastination with extra steps. |

## Exit criteria

A stated situation, relevant history surfaced, a recommended frame — and the human recognizes their situation in your description. Then, if action is warranted: `/decide`.
